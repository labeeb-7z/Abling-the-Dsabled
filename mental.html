Sarthak Educational Trust is a Non Government Organization which is working towards the empowerment of people with disability.
Inclusion, Empowerment, and Mainstreaming of Persons with disability is the guiding principle of Sarthak Educational Trust.
Many kids have been rehabilitated through its 'Early Intervention' and 'Inclusive Education' initiatives.

How they assist :
Sarthak has a number of programs designed for different specifics of people

  • Medical Rehabilitation of Children with special needs with the help of trained therapists.
  • Building an inclusive ecosystem with mainstreaming children with special needs in regular schools.
  • 3 - month free of cost Vocational Skill training and Employment Program for persons with disability.
  • Helping the persons with disability to build a sustainable livelihood through employment opportunities in various IT/ITes, hospitality and retail-based companies.
  • Vocational skill competitions for persons with disability at National, Regional and International level.

AMC is a NGO that supports over 200 children, adolescents and adults.
AMC’s aim is to educate, train and rehabilitate individuals with intellectual disability disorder (IDD) and helps them achieve a sense of empowerment.
AMC also extends support to the families of these individuals and handholds them in their journey of caring for their wards with special needs.

How they assist :

  • Special School Provides special education for children with IDD who are between 6 and 16 years of age and equips them with life skills.
  • Multi-category Vocational Training Center Trains adolescents and adults with IDD in various vocations
  • AMC Sheltered workshop Offers gainful employment to the adults who have acquired skills and are able to work under minimum supervision.
  • Day Care Centre Cares for 15 to 20 severely affected individuals who are engaged through various activities that keeps them calm and helps them with their occupational therapy
  • Child guidance clinic A team consisting of psychiatrists, psychologists and social workers provide comprehensive assessment and guidance. They address the needs of the beneficiaries, the staff and the parents of beneficiaries.
  • Parents Self Help Group: Parents of beneficiaries at AMC have formed a self–help group which works to mobilise resources for medicines, organises sports day at AMC and also facilitates various benefits from the State and the Central Government like NIRAMAYA Scheme.
